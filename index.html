deBridge: The Interoperability Standard for Web3

About the deBridge Protocol

deBridge is a decentralized protocol and infrastructure platform that enables the seamless and secure transfer of assets and data between various blockchains. Our mission is to solve the critical problem of liquidity and information fragmentation in the Web3 ecosystem, providing a unified and secure layer for cross-chain communication. This repository serves as a hub for our core protocol development, security audits, and technical documentation. We believe in complete transparency and an open-source approach to building the future of decentralized finance.

Core Principles and Vision

The core vision behind deBridge is to eliminate the 'walled gardens' of individual blockchains. By creating a robust and secure interoperability layer, we enable assets and value to flow freely across different chains, unlocking unprecedented possibilities for decentralized applications. Our architecture is built on the principles of **Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT)**, ensuring that the network remains secure even if some validators are malicious.

The security of our users' assets is our highest priority. We maintain a continuous and rigorous security posture, including regular third-party audits and a public bug bounty program.

Frequently Asked Questions (T2)

Here are answers to some of the most common questions about deBridge.

Q1: What blockchains does deBridge support?

A1: deBridge is designed to be a multi-chain protocol. The current list of supported networks includes Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon, Arbitrum, Solana, and more. Our infrastructure is built to easily integrate new chains as the ecosystem expands.

Q2: What is the main difference between deBridge and other cross-chain bridges?

A2: Unlike many other bridges that rely on a 'lock-and-mint' model, deBridge uses a different approach for asset transfers. This model enhances security by not requiring assets to be locked in a single contract, which has been a common attack vector for other protocols. Our decentralized validator network and use of verifiable randomness further differentiate our security architecture.

Q3: How does the deBridge validator network work?

A3: Our validators, who are elected by the protocol's governance, run a dedicated deBridge node. When a cross-chain transaction is initiated, these validators independently track and sign the transaction. The destination smart contract then requires a minimum number of valid signatures from the network to execute the transaction, ensuring that it is secure and validated by a decentralized consensus.

Q4: How does deBridge ensure randomness and fairness for certain functions?

A4: For applications requiring verifiable randomness, such as leader elections or lottery systems, deBridge can integrate with decentralized services like Chainlink VRF. This provides a cryptographically secure and auditable source of randomness that cannot be tampered with by any single entity.
